## Manufacturing Excellence

<img width="1919" height="954" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/7c846b58-f8ca-4c4e-b94b-f54edac4bb79" />

- Now let's put everything together and talk about manufacturing excellence. So the question of which manufacturing strategy is right for your company, it really has to fit into the whole supply chain that you have. Internally, we always talk about source, make, and deliver. It also has to match up with a corporate strategy. So whatever the company wants to accomplish that has to match up from source, to make, to deliver. So there's nothing necessarily too specific that makes your manufacturing stands out. But ultimately, the goal has to be execute the overall corporate strategy.
- However, that is accomplished, that's the right manufacturing strategy forward. But lastly, whether a company uses one strategy versus another generically, it's not necessarily that one strategy done well is significantly better than another strategy executed equally well. It's about what one of my friends used to call doing the right things right. So if you take a strategy and you really apply it correctly and you focus on execution, and you perform well, it's going to lead to good outcomes. If you use the perfect strategy but you executed poorly, then it's not going to lead to good outcomes. So ultimately, it is important to pick the right strategy, but execution of that strategy is more important in my opinion. You don't want to focus too much time on picking the perfect strategy and then not have enough resources to execute it, or pick a strategy that might seem like it should work well for you and you can't execute. So ultimately, you have to do the right things within your environment and perform them to the best of your abilities.
